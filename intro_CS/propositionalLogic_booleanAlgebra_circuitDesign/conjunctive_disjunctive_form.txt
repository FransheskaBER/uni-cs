CONJUNCTIVE NORMAL FORM (CNF) - ANDs of ORs
- It is a conjunction of disjunctions 
- AND connects groups of ORs: (p ∨ ¬q) ∧ (r ∨ s)
- The example above has two groups of disjunctions (ORs) - (p ∨ ¬q) and (r ∨ s) and they are
    connected by AND ∧
- In java, the example above will look like: (p || !q) && (r || s)
- more examples:
    (a ∨ b) ∧ (c ∨ d) - CNF
    (a ∧ b) ∨ (c ∧ d) - NO CNF
    (a ∨ b ∨ c) ∧ (d) - CNF (single literal also qualifies as trivial disjunction)
    a ∧ b ∧ c         - CNF (single literals connected by ANDs)

DISJUNCTIVE NORMAL FORM (DNF) - ORs of ANDs
- This is the opositve of disjunction of conjunctions meaning:
- OR connects groups of ANDs: (p ∧ q) ∨ (¬r ∧ ¬s)
- In java: (p && q) || (!r && !s)
- more examples:
    (a ∧ b) ∨ (c ∧ d) - DNF
    (a ∨ b) ∧ (c ∨ d) - NO DNF
    a ∨ b ∨ c         - DNF (single literals connected by ORs)
    (a ∧ b ∧ c) ∨ d   - DNF


SATISFIABILITY (does at least one input combination make the the formula true?)
- A formula is satisfiable if at least one combination of inputs makes it true.
- For example: the formula p ∨ ¬p is always true hence it is satisfiable
    build a trueh table:
    p || NOTp
    T || F -> T
    F || T -> T
